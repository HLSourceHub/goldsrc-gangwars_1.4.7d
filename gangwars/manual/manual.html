GANGWARS 1.4

User’s Manual

Synopsis:

G angwars first appeared on LAN servers early 1998, and then released to the public on Mar 29 of the same year as a Quake 2 add on. Nobody back then caught up to the realism concept, and it went unnoticed. Four years go by, and a wide range of players have accepted the fun factor of ballistic weaponry and realism as 'the way to go'. Tera-joule plasma guns are no longer as threatening as a German H&K MP5; and this was obviously bound to happen. Demographics show that the average age of the online game player has skewed dramatically with time as the mature crowd settled in. Mods based on realism, whether they'd be militaristic or law enforcement, have become the de facto templates to follow in the online gaming community. Gangwars was obviously targeting the wrong crowd in 1998, but the fun factor parameters have changed to its benefit and what better time to bring back an age-long concept holding key features of today's most wanted playability list, to a community saturated with cliché mods trying to replicate Saving Private Ryan's Omaha beach session? It would be a sin not to release the mod now, when the Half-Life engine is most fertile and enjoyable.

Teams:

Upon connecting to a Gangwars server, you are expected to join one of the two teams – East Coast and West Coast. The east coast team members wear dark pants, and the west coast team members wear light pants. As a member of a team, you are expected to protect your teammates from the opposing attacks. You are also expected to exterminate all enemy team members.

Goal:

The goal of the game is to exterminate all enemy team members in the allotted round time. The enemy also shares the same goal, so offensive and defensive tactics are crucial. One important aspect of this goal is the hunting and killing of the enemy team boss. The boss is tracked by radar on your display and exterminating him prevents the enemy from re-spawning (see Bosses and bags).

Bosses and bags:

A boss is a team member that has found the narcotics (boss) bag, and has decided to carry the bag around. The bags are usually around each team’s spawn points. Once a boss, you have the ability to heal yourself and nearby teammates, buy weapons and ammo at any time, and re-spawn your dead teammates as a result of a successful enemy kill. By being a boss, you also have the duty to flee enemy attacks as they can track you through radar. The right strategy is to have a good portion of the teammates protect their boss, while the remaining members hunt the enemy boss.

Navigation:

A radar display allows you to keep track of your boss as well as the enemy boss. The green arrow tracks your boss; the red arrow tracks the enemy boss. If there is no boss, then the arrow will point to the last location of the boss bag. All players can buy ammo and weapons near their spawns or their armory. If you are near an armory, your HUD money will turn green, indicating the possibility of a new purchase. If the armory closes after a certain time, your HUD will lose the ‘A’ character next to the timer. Once the armory is closed, you cannot buy anything any more, unless you are a boss.

Roles:

Hunters and bodyguards are the two main roles of the ordinary team members. Bosses have the roles of being bosses, and their job is to heal and re-spawn their teammates. The hunters usually track the red arrow on their radar screen, trying to locate and exterminate the enemy boss. A good weapon of choice for hunters is the single-shot rifle. Hunters can also go solo, without any reinforcement, and hide in strategic places, and wait for the enemy boss. The bodyguards are expected to track and stay with the green arrow, which points to the current position of their boss. The bodyguards require the use of heavy weaponry for maximum damage in their radius. The bodyguards will also benefit from an automatic healing if they are close enough to their boss.

Weapons:

Different weapons are adequate for different situations. At the beginning of every round, you start with a pistol and a knife. Surely, the low firing rate, and short range of the pistol won’t take you too far before you lay dead somewhere. The first five seconds of every round allow all players on the server to take their time and buy enough ammo and weapons to have a good fight. The choice of weapons can make a difference depending on the environment and the goal you are trying to achieve. The most expensive guns are not always the best choices. You need to know your weapons and understand how they react to different aspects such as single shot, burst, automatic fire, jump fire, crouch fire, concentration fire, etc. Also, learn where to shoot the enemy - from the front, from the back, in the head, in the leg, etc. (See Appendix A)

Reloading weapons:

Knowing when to reload weapons is very important, as you need to understand that once you slide a clip out, the remaining bullets on that clip are forever lost. Make sure that reload only when necessary; with time this will not be an issue for you.

Healing:

Every injured teammate within a 15 feet radius of the boss will automatically be healed. The boss also heals himself at a slightly faster rate. When attacking an enemy boss, make sure that you don’t leave until you kill him; otherwise, your effort would be futile, since your target’s health regenerates at a relatively fast rate. This is where a single-shot rifle comes handy.

Money:

At the end of every round, you are awarded some money. The amount of money you will receive depends highly on your personal performance, and slightly on your team’s performance in that round. The money allows you to purchase higher caliber weaponry. (See Appendix A)

The HUD:

The Head Up Display shows your status. Please refer to the picture below for more information:

BLUE – 2d and 3d radars keep track of the bosses

RED – Armory icon ‘A’ - This icon goes off when armory is closed.

YELLOW – Time remaining in round

GREEN - Health

CYAN – Target ID

PURPLE – Available money - This icon goes green when purchase is available.

WHITE – Available ammo in weapon and clips

ORANGE – Shows the boss status

Administrator Commands:

The following commands are available from the GUI to authenticated administrators. The title of the menu refers to the client subject to harassment by the administrator:

UnFreeze/ Freeze:

Freeze selected player. Player will not be able to move

UnGag/Gag :

Gag selected player. Player will not be able to chat

Smack:

Slap selected player with some damage

Kick:

Requires Admin Level 1 and up

Kick player out of the server

Ban 5 min, 30 min, 60 min, 1 day, 1 week, for life:

Requires Admin Level 2 and up

Ban selected player for the selected time period

Level 3 administrators with cannot be banned with this command

Name bitch:

Rename selected player to “bitch”

Name cheater:

Rename selected player to “cheater”

Smoke East:

Requires Admin Level 3 and up

Kill all members of the East Coast team

Smoke West:

Requires Admin Level 3 and up

Kill all members of the West Coast team

Reset Scores:

Requires Admin Level 3 and up

Reset map and player scores

Server Settings (CVARS):

The following are cvars available to authenticated server administrators with rcon or local access:

gw_pass0 <password>

Set admin level 0 password (slap)

gw_pass1 <password>

Set admin level 1 password (kick)

gw_pass2 <password>

Set admin level 2 password (kick ban)

gw_pass3 <password>

Set admin level 3 password (kick ban/exterminate)

gw_ping_min < # msec>

Maximum ping (in msec) required to stay on server

gw_ping_rounds <# rounds>

Number of rounds over which to resample pings

gw_ping_ban <# minutes>

Number of minutes the HPB will be banned, 0 for no ban

gw_mapvote_ratio <percent>

Minimum percentage of players voting the same map before a map change

gw_mapvote <0|1>

Toggle map voting ON/OFF

<password>

Authenticate user as level X if password put in console matches gw_passX
